## THE THESIS: WHY THIS EXISTS

Every is not a media company. Every is not a product company. Every is a **conviction company** — and conviction travels through people before it ever travels through ads.

The growth loop looks like this:

```
Personal Conviction → Public Insight → Trust → Attribution → 
Product Curiosity → Usage → Better Conviction → Better Insight
```

This is a **compounding engine**, but most companies treat their employees' public voices as either:
1. **Untapped** ("I don't post enough")
2. **Over-tapped** ("Every tweet is a CTA")
3. **Invisible** ("I don't know where I fit")

**Surface IQ** is the spatial awareness layer. We're not telling anyone when to post or what to say. We're showing the floor. Where the lanes are. Where the defense collapses. Where your natural game already works — and where an open surface might be waiting.

This is inspired by the same analytics philosophy that tracks shot selection, gravity, and spacing: **efficiency without ego, volume without waste, and team chemistry over individual scoring.**

---

## THE VISUAL LANGUAGE

We borrowed from ESPN's player tracking and NBA analytics to make this feel familiar, lightweight, and shareable:

- **Shot Charts:** Radar maps showing which surfaces each player currently occupies
- **Badges:** "The Floor General," "The Sharpshooter," etc. — archetypes, not rankings
- **Splits:** Surface-by-surface presence (not performance metrics we can't see, but *presence* we can)
- **Plus/Minus:** Notes on what each player *adds* to the team chemistry, not what they lack

**The Golden Rule:** No leaderboards. No "who's best." Everyone has something they're the best at. The goal is making that visible so the team learns from each other.
